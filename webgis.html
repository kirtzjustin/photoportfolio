J. Kirtz Travel & Photography

As a GIS professional and developer, travel enthusiast, and hobbyist photographer I can think of no better way to keep track of my journey across the nation and world than developing my own GIS application. As I travel to new places I, rather obsessively, log my location into Google Maps' Saved Places and then periodically convert them to a shapefile for use in this application. I've also logged the location of some of my favorite personal photographs. Join me as I travel the USA and our world!

Technologies Used: ESRI JavaScript API 4.x, ArcGIS Online, Bootstrap, Sass, jQuery, Chart.js, Tabulator

About Me

After growing up in Charlotte, North Carolina I decided to get a change of scenery from city life and attended Appalachian State University in rural Boone, North Carolina. Having a life-long interest in science, I majored in Geography with a concentration in GIS (Geographic Information Systems). Upon graduating college in 2010 I served as the GIS Planner for the City of Greer, SC where I was a one man GIS department. During this time I discovered a passion for writing code and taught myself Python and JavaScript in my spare time at home. I oversaw the implementation and development of Greer's web GIS platform using ArcGIS Server and wrote Greer's custom web-based GIS viewer using the ESRI JavaScript 4.x API. After seven years with Greer I decided to transition to the private sector and joined SynTerra Corporation in downtown Greenville, SC as the GIS Administrator specializing in GIS web development. After three years with SynTerra I decided to return to the public sector as the GIS Supervisor with Greenville Water, where I lead the GIS team within the Department of Engineering.


The Greer Citizen, March 14, 2018

I currently live in the Greenville area with my wife Allison and our beautiful daughter, Scarlett. We also have two snakes; Slinky, a corn snake and Atheris, an eastern kingsnake.
